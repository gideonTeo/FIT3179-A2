In ordinary language, terrorism is defined as the use of violence to instil fear in a society in order to achieve a specific political goal. The inherent ferocity and violence of terrorism has developed an intense stigma. Terrorism has been perpetrated by right-wing and left-wing political organisations, nationalistic and religious groups, revolutionaries, and even official agencies including militaries, intelligence services, and police (Jenkins, n.d.).

Trend of Terrorism

The line chart shows the total terrorism cases over the world from 2007 to 2017. We can see that the overall trend of total terrorism cases increased. There is a plateau from 2007 to 2011 followed by an obvious increasing trend from 2011 to 2014. Then it decreases gradually from 2014 to 2017.

Note that the total cases depending on the data collected from Kaggle Dataset. There may be some missing data for some countries due to data limitations. Therefore, total terrorism cases vary with countries.

If you want to know the total terrorism cases specifically in each year, tooltip is available.

Terrorism Index by Country

On the map, higher terrorism cases are shown with darker shades. We can see that Iraq has the highest terrorism cases due its darker colours.

Using the bar chart below by selecting the continent of "Asia", you can see that Iraq is reflected as highest in Asia followed by Pakistan.

Also, certain parts of the map is greyed out because of our limited dataset that does not cover all countries.

Top 10 Countries

The bar chart on the left shows the Top 10 countries with the highest terrorism index.

As it may be difficult to tell Iraq has the highest terrorism index in the map, a bar chart is created to ease reader to find out which country with the highest terrorism index. There is a filter option for reader to explore on terrorism index in different continent.

Tooltip is also available on both map and bar chart.

Feel free to select different continents to see the top 10 terrorism index in each continent.

Attack type happened in Iraq

The top bar chart on the right shows the type of attack happened in Iraq over years. The bottom bar chart shows the total number of attack happened for each type over years.

From the bar chart, we can see that 2014 has the highest total number of cases in Iraq compared to the rest. In terms of attack type (second bar chart), the category "Bombing/Explosion" has the highest total count compared to the rest of the category.

If you would loke to explore the data further, click on the category (attack type) you would like to explore of the bar chart below to see how each attack changes over the years. You will be able to explore the total attack happened for each category over the years.

Tooltip is also available on both bar charts.

This visualisation is inspired by Plotly (Plotly, n.d.).
